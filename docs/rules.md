# Okey Rules (Traditional play)

This document describes the rules our team will implement based on how we play Okey.

---
## 1. Objective

The goal of the game is to separate all tiles in a player's hand into valid groups in order to win the round and minimize points.

Valid groups include:

- **Run**: At least 3 consecutive numbers of the same color.
- **Set**: At least 3 tiles of the same number in different colors.

Points are calculated based on the numerical value of the tiles.
For example, a set of three 3s is worth 9 points (3 × 3).

## 2. Tiles, Rounds, and Players 
- There are 106 tiles in the game (including 2 jokers). 
  - There are **two sets**  of each numbered tile **1–13**, i.e., two copies of every number from 1-13.
  - Colors of tiles are orange, black, blue, and red.
- An entire game typically consists of **6 rounds**.
- The game is played by four players.

---

## 3. Dealing and Starting a Round
- All tiles are dealt face down. Each player may view only their own tiles.
- Each player receives **14 tiles**, except for **one starting player** who receives **15 tiles** to begin the game.
  - This starting player will throw away one of their tiles. Discarded tiles for all players are placed face up and remain visible.
  - The turn then goes to the player on his right (game is played counter-clockwise).
---

## 4. Turn Structure

During a turn:

- A player draws one tile:
  - From the **face-down draw_tile pile**, or
  - From the **previously discarded (visible) tile** of the player before them, provided they are opening their hand using that tile (see Opening Requirements below).
- The player may arrange or play tiles according to the game rules.
- The player must discard one tile to end their turn.
- After discarding, the player must again have exactly **14 tiles** in hand.
---

## 5. Opening Requirements

### 5.1 Standard Opening
- A player must have a minimum of **81 total points** to open their hand for the first time in a round. Tiles are placed on the table. 
- Multiple players can open during a round. 
  - If a player opens after another player has already opened, they must open with a value that **exceeds the previous opening value**.
- A player who has already opened their hand may add tiles to another player’s opened melds, provided the tiles follow the standard game rules (i.e., extending a consecutive run of the same color or adding a different color of the same number to a set).

### 5.2 Star Bonus for Strong Opening
- A player earns **one star** if they are **the first to open** and their opening is of **100+ value**.
- A star results in **-100 points** applied to that player’s total score.
- Other players must still exceed the current opening requirement, but **only the first opener can earn the star**.

---

## 6. Finishing the Hand

### 6.1 Standard Finish
There are **two ways** a player may finish a round:

#### A. Finish by Discarding
- To win (finish a round), a player must get rid of all their tiles, including discarding their final tile.
#### B. Finish with a Complete Hand (No Discard)
- A player may finish the round if all tiles in their hand form valid groups according to the game rules. In this case, the player finishes with 15 tiles and does not discard a final tile.

### 6.2 Opponent Scoring When a Player Finishes

- The finishing player receives **0 points** for the round.

Opponent scoring depends on how the round is finished.

#### A. If the Player Finishes by Discarding


  - Players who **did not open** during the round receive **100 points**.
  - Players who **did open** receive their **current board tally** (the value of the tiles remaining in their hand).

#### B. If the Player Finishes with a Complete Hand (No Discard)

- Opponent scoring follows the same rules as finishing by discarding a non-joker tile:
  - Players who **did not open** receive **100 points**.
  - Players who **did open** receive their remaining board tally.
---
## 7. Winning

- After 6 rounds, total scores accumulated per round are summed.
- Any star bonuses are applied (subtract star points as defined above).
- The player with the **lowest total score** wins the match.
